About Me


I’m Ali Aboelela, a 4th year computer engineering student at the University of Toronto. I was born in Egypt but lived in Saudi Arabia until 2019 when I moved to Toronto.

I have a technical interest in Robotics & AI. I think they represent the reason why I got into software engineering well; automating tasks for the sake of convenience, or more importantly because machines are often more efficient or capable than us.

I would like to work in the healthcare, energy, or agriculture industries. I'm interested in healthcare because it's extremely rewarding to work on improving others' health outcomes. There's also some really cool tech ranging from things like the da Vinci machine to low cost diagnostics. My interest in energy & agriculture stems from the belief that (against the back drop of climate change) these industries face significant challenges in the next 50-100 years and will likely see major changes in their production, transport/transmission, storage, and other processes.

Outside of university and work I enjoy everything to do with nature and the outdoors. Growing up my family was fortunate enough to travel around the world. I’ve inherited my parents' spirit for travel and am always looking forward to the next landscape which will take my breath away.
